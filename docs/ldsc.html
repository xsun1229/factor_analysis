Introduction

I adopted LD score regression to estimate SNP-based heritability and partation the heritability into seperate categories. The software I used here is ldsc.

Material and Methods

‘ldsc’ takes GWAS summary statistics, LD scores, regression weights, and allele frequenciesas inputs. Regression weights, allele frequenciesas and 53 categories of LD scroes can be downloaded here. We need to prepare our own GWAS summary statistics and LD scores.

LD scores

I used 13 histone marks (H3K27ac,DNase,H3K27me3,H3K36me3,H3K4me1,H3K4me3,H3K9me3,H3K9ac,H2A,H3K4me2,H3K79me2,H4K20me1,H3K9me1) and open chromatin regions in immune cells to compute partitioned LD scores. The marks and cell types are listed below.

Summary Statistics

The software ‘ldsc’ uses GWAS summary statistics as inputs. The GWAS summary statistics we adopted here came from the association tests for factors ~ variants + covariants(using training data, 536 samples). I performed the test for both maf>0.05 variants and maf>0.01 variants. The covariants here are 1.top ten pcs from genotype matrix 2.factors whose heritability equal to 0.000001 and p_val_test_l is greater than 0.5.

I converted the GWAS results into following format(taking lv73, the one has the highest heritability in plier factors_allpathway as an example).

The ‘SNP’ IDs here are from dbSNP 151, I downloaded the correspondence between dbSNP 151 and GTEx variant ID from the GTEx portal

The ‘N’ here stands for the the number of non-missing samples for each variants.

‘Z’ is the z-score for each variants. I computed it from p-value and effect size. Z=sign(Effect Size)×|phi^(-1)(p/2)|. phi^(-1) is the inverse cumulative distribution function of the normal distribution.

A1 is the alternative allele and A2 is the reference allele.
